Topic: Machine Learning Operations (MLOps) Lifecycle
MLOps is the strategic integration of data engineering, machine learning, and DevOps to streamline the transition of models from research prototypes to robust production systems. Unlike traditional software, ML models require continuous management because they are dependent on the statistical properties of the data they process.

The core lifecycle stages include:

Data Ingestion: Establishing automated pipelines to collect, clean, and validate raw data from multiple sources.

Training: Developing reproducible training workflows where hyperparameters and code versions are tracked.

Evaluation: Testing models against held-out datasets to ensure performance metrics meet business benchmarks before deployment.

Deployment: Serving models through APIs or batch processing systems, ensuring high availability and low latency.

Monitoring: Tracking production performance to identify "Data Drift"—a phenomenon where the input data in the real world evolves over time (e.g., consumer behavior changes), causing the model's accuracy to degrade.

When drift is detected, the MLOps pipeline triggers automated retraining, creating a closed-loop system that ensures the model remains relevant without manual intervention. This framework is the foundation of scalable, professional data science.
