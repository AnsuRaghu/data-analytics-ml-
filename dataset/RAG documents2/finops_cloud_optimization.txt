FinOps is the collaborative practice of managing cloud costs to maximize business value. As cloud usage scales, organizations often face "cost spirals" due to over-provisioning.

Key FinOps strategies include:

Rightsizing: Analyzing actual usage metrics to scale compute instances down to the appropriate size.

Spot Instances: Utilizing spare cloud capacity for non-critical batch analytics at a fraction of the cost.

Budget Alerting: Setting automated thresholds to receive notifications when spend approaches a limit.

Effective FinOps changes the culture of an engineering team from "always-on" to "cost-aware," ensuring that data analytics pipelines remain financially sustainable as they grow from a proof-of-concept to a large-scale production system
